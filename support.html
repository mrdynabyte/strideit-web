FAQ

Do I need an Apple Watch to use Stride It?

Yes. Stride It uses your Apple Watch as the primary step-tracking sensor. The iPhone companion app displays your data and provides insights, but the Watch app must be installed on your wrist to record steps.

Compatible with Apple Watch Series 4 and later running watchOS 10 or above.

How do I install the app on my Apple Watch?

After downloading Stride It on your iPhone, open the Watch app on your iPhone, scroll to find Stride It under Available Apps, and tap Install. Once installed, the Stride It app will appear on your Apple Watch.

You can also follow the in-app onboarding wizard — it guides you through this process step by step.

Why are my widgets not updating?

iOS controls when widgets refresh based on battery level and usage patterns. To help widgets update more reliably:

  • Make sure Background App Refresh is enabled for Stride It in Settings → Stride It.
  • Keep your iPhone and Apple Watch within Bluetooth range regularly throughout the day.
  • Opening the app briefly will always force an immediate widget update.
Does Stride It use HealthKit?

Yes. Stride It reads step data from Apple HealthKit, which aggregates data from your Apple Watch. We request read-only access to your step count. No personal data is stored on external servers — everything stays on your device and in your iCloud account.

When do step counts reset?

Step counts reset automatically at midnight (12:00 AM) according to your iPhone's local time zone, following Apple HealthKit's standard daily cycle. No extra configuration is needed.

Can I change the app language?

Yes. Stride It defaults to the language of your device's region, but you can override it at any time. Go to the Settings tab inside the app and choose your preferred language from the list.

Currently supported languages: English and Spanish.

How do I turn off or customize notifications?

Stride It sends three daily reminders (7 AM, 12 PM, and 5:30 PM) with your remaining step count for the day. To manage or disable them, go to iOS Settings → Notifications → Stride It.

Notifications also appear on your Apple Watch when it is on your wrist.

What is the step goal? Can I change it?

The default daily goal is 8,800 steps — a research-backed target shown to deliver meaningful cardiovascular benefits. You can update your personal goal from the Settings tab in the app.
